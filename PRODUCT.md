# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary audiences:**
- **Tourists** planning visits to Granada — first-time and returning visitors researching what to do, where to go, and what's happening
- **Locals** discovering current events, hidden gems, and fresh perspectives on their own city

**Context:** Users arrive with intent — they want to know what's worth their time. The site must earn trust quickly through design quality, then deliver practical information (events, routes, guides) without friction.

## Product Purpose

Granada Urban is a cultural and intelligent guide for Granada, Spain. It surfaces curated events, walking routes, time-based itineraries, and practical guides — all with a distinct visual identity that elevates the city beyond generic tourism.

**Success looks like:** A visitor opens the site and immediately feels they're in the right place. The aesthetic communicates "this is made by people who love Granada" rather than "this is a template." Information is findable, scannable, and visually memorable.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Warm, editorial, Mediterranean

**Voice:** Confident but not arrogant. Locally rooted, not tourist-bureau. The design should feel like a beautifully printed city guide you'd find in a boutique hotel — not a government brochure or a blog.

**Emotional goals:**
- Discovery (there's always something new)
- Belonging (this guide "gets" Granada)
- Calm confidence (quality curation over noise)

## Anti-references

- **Generic travel-blog templates** — no cookie-cutter card grids, no stock hero images, no "10 best things to do" listicles dressed in a purchased theme
- **Official government tourism sites** — no flat bureaucracy aesthetic, no cold institutional language, no overwhelming density of links
- **Personality-free UI** — no safe, watered-down design that could be any city anywhere. The visual identity must be unmistakably Granada

## Design Principles

1. **Show, don't tell** — the design itself should communicate Granada's character (architecture, light, material) before the user reads a word
2. **Quality is the filter** — every visual choice signals curation. Nothing should look templated, placeholder, or rushed
3. **Dark for drama, light for reading** — the hero and portal moments are cinematic and dark; the content body is warm, legible, and editorial
4. **Local first, not tourist-first** — speak to people who live here or wish they did. Avoid generic travel copy
5. **Motion as atmosphere** — animations are ambient and cinematic, never performative or distracting

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AAA** target for contrast and legibility wherever achievable
- **Reduced motion** alternatives for all animations and parallax effects
- **Color blindness** consideration: never rely on color alone for difficulty indicators or category tags (always paired with icon or text)
- **Keyboard navigation** fully supported for all interactive elements (filters, navigation, overlays)
